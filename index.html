Introduction

This is a database of all metrical units (passages in an individual meter) in the extant plays of Plautus and Terence except for the fragmentary Vidularia.

Each record represents a metrical unit, or a passage in a single meter (these range from one verse to over 200 verses) and includes the following fields:

  • Playwright
  • Play
  • Line # first: the number of the first verse of this metrical unit
  • Line # last: number of the last verse of this metrical unit
  • Number of lines: total number of verses in this metrical unit
  • Line first: a quotation of the first verse in the passage
  • Line last: a quotation of the last verse in the passage
  • Meter: the meter of the passage (in the case of verses that include more than one meter, all are recorded as one: e.g.: ba2bacol counts as one meter, as does a versus reizianus and cr2tr4cat)
  • Type: the type of meter to which the meter of the passage belongs (if two or more meter types occur in the same verse, both are counted: e.g.: cr2tr4cat counts as both cretic and trochaic)
  • Line Before: the meter of the passage immediately preceding this one
  • Line After: the meter of the passage immediately following this one
  • Closure: Notes on how the passage ends (e.g., whether the end of the metrical unit corresponds with the end of a sentence or a scene)
  • Comments on Length: remarks on such matters as numbers of long and short syllables, resolutions, and variations on standard metrical forms
  • Character, Type, Num of Lines: The name of each character who speaks or sings in the passage, the character’s type, and the number of verses in which that character speaks or sings in the passage. In verses where one or more characters speak, one verse is attributed to each character, regardless of how much or how little of the verse that character delivers.
  • Comments (Other): notes on textual questions, content, choice of meter, and other matters.

How to Use the Database

The interface for the database is comprised of three main sections: the dimension controls, the facet controls, and the results list. There are eight individual dimensions: "Playwright", "Play", "Character Type", "Character", "Meter", "Meter Type", "Meter Before", and "Meter After". Each of these eight dimenmsions contain two or more facets (these can also be thought of inversely as filters). Each facet can either be selected or deselected. While selected, the results list will include records where the dimension contains the selected facet. While deselected, those records will be filtered out of the results list.

To select or deselect a particular facet, click the title of desired dimension, then click the desired facet. When the facet is selected its background will show a green color. When deselected the facet's background will be a red color. You can click the title of the dimension again to collapse the list.

To select or deselect many facets at once, a list of dimension controls is available near the top of the page. Each dimension has an associated "Select All" and "Deselect All" option. These can be used to select or deselect all facets for that dimension at once. There is also an control for selecting or deselecting all facets of all dimensions at once. This is listed as its own control labeled "Dimensions".

Additional information about a particular record's notes is also available through clicking the "Notes" link within the "Extras" column of the results list.

Sources

Except where otherwise noted, the text and scansion of the verses described in this database derive from the following sources:

Plautus:

Text and scansion of all verses included in Caesar Questa (ed.), T. Macci Plauti Cantica (Urbino: QuattroVenti, 1995) derive from that work.

For passages of Plautus not included in Questa’s text:

Text: Friedrich Leo (ed.), Plauti Comoediae (Berlin: Weidmann, 1895-1896). Metrical identification: Wallace M. Lindsay (ed.), T. Macci Plauti Comoediae (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1904-1905)

Terence:

Text and metrical identification: Robert Kauer and Wallace M. Lindsay (eds.), P. Terenti Afri Comoediae (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1926, reprinted with additions by Otto Skutsch, 1958).

Decisions I made regarding the authenticity of questioned verses are noted throughout the database in the “Comments (other)” field of the “Versus browse form” layout. Note in particular that I count both alternate endings of Poenulus as interpolations, and I did not count Stichus 48-57. I did not count the Vidularia or the fragments not attributed to extant plays, but I did count the fragments of Amphitruo, Aulularia, Bacchides, and Cistellaria.
